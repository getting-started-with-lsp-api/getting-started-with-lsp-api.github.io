Basic Commands

Commonly used commands

These are the most commonly used commands for the FLL coding:

  • move.forward_for
  • move.backward_for
  • move.forward_to_red_border
  • move.forward_to_blue_border
  • move.right_motor_left_for
  • move.right_motor_right_for
  • move.right_motor_left_for
  • move.right_motor_right_for
  • front_arm.run_for_degrees
  • breakpoint

Refer to the below sections to understand the above commands

How to's for moving the robot

How to move forward/backward for specific cm or inch

To move forward/backward 10 cm at a speed of 300: 


      move.forward_for(10, "cm", 300, 300)
      move.backward_for(10, "cm", 300, 300)

To move forward/backward 10 inch at a speed of 300:


    move.forward_for(10, "in", 300, 300)
    move.backward_for(10, "in", 300, 300)

move.backward_for is similar to move.forward_for but in the opposite direction. ⚠Note that the allowed speed is 0-650, so 300 is ~50% speed.

How to move forward/backward to red home border at speed of 300

To move forward/backward to red border at a speed of 300: 


     move.forward_to_red_border(300, 300)
     move.backward_to_red_border(300, 300)

How to move forward/backward to blue home border at speed of 300

To move forward/backward to blue border at a speed of 300: 


       move.forward_to_blue_border(300, 300)
       move.backward_to_blue_border(300, 300)

How to turn to a specific yaw angle

⚠ Note that you want to use a slow speed(max 100) for turns to have a higher accuracy. Pre-Steps:
  • Decide what yaw angle you need to move to. (You need to check hub properties page by clicking the hub icon in the top-left to find this out).
  • Decide whether you want to move the left or right wheel/motor.

To turn the right motor in the left direction to achieve a yaw angle of 150, at a speed of 100: 


    move.right_motor_left_for(100, 150)

To turn the right motor in the right direction to achieve a yaw angle of 50, at a speed of 80: 


    move.right_motor_right_for(80, 50)

To turn the left motor in the left direction to achieve a yaw angle of 150, at a speed of 100: 


    move.left_motor_left_for(100, 150)

To turn the left motor in the right direction to achieve a yaw angle of 50, at a speed of 80: 


    move.left_motor_right_for(80, 50)

How to's for turning the robot arms

How to turn the front robot arm

⚠ Note that you should use a slow speed(max 100) for better accuracy. ⚠ Note that since we are using geared motors, clockwise on the motor turns into counter-clockwise movement of the arm.

To turn the front robot arm by 140 degrees clockwise at a speed of 100: 


  front_arm.run_for_degrees(140, 100)

To turn the front robot arm by 140 degrees counter-clockwise at a speed of 100: 


  front_arm.run_for_degrees(140, -100)

How to's for debugging

How to set a breakpoint

To set a breakpoint activated by the left button: 


  breakpoint(button=button.LEFT)
To set a breakpoint activated by the right button: 

  breakpoint(button=button.RIGHT)
If you add a breakpoint, the robot will pause execution at that point until you press the specified button. This is very useful for debugging.

How to set the hub power button color

To set the hub power button to the color RED: 


  hub.light.color(hub.light.POWER, RED)
This is useful for debugging to figure out which step in the code you are at.

How to's for debugging

How to print a message to the Spike Prime Console

To print a message "Step 1" to the Spike Prime Console: 


  print("Step 1")
This is useful for debugging to figure out which step in the code you are at.

How to go to the end of the file without scrolling

To go to the end of the file without scrolling: Press Ctrl + G A message such as this will be displayed: Current Line: 1, Character: 38. Type a line number between 1 and 321 to navigate to. Note the highest line number, 321 in this case and type that.
