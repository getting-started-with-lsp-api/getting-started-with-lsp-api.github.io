Basic Commands

Commonly used commands

These are the most commonly used commands for the FLL coding:

  • move.forward_for
  • move.backward_for
  • move.forward_to_red_border
  • move.forward_to_blue_border
  • move.right_motor_left_for
  • move.right_motor_right_for
  • move.right_motor_left_for
  • move.right_motor_right_for
  • front_arm.run_for_degrees
  • breakpoint

Refer to the below sections to understand the above commands

How to move forward/backward for specific cm or inch

To move forward/backward 10 cm at a speed of 300: 


      move.forward_for(10, "cm", 300, 300)
      move.backward_for(10, "cm", 300, 300)

To move forward/backward 10 inch at a speed of 300:


    move.forward_for(10, "in", 300, 300)
    move.backward_for(10, "in", 300, 300)

move.backward_for is similar to move.forward_for but in the opposite direction. ⚠Note that the allowed speed is 0-650, so 300 is ~50% speed.

How to move forward/backward to red home border at speed of 300

To move forward/backward to red border at a speed of 300: 


     move.forward_to_red_border(300, 300)
     move.backward_to_red_border(300, 300)

How to move forward/backward to blue home border at speed of 300

To move forward/backward to blue border at a speed of 300: 


       move.forward_to_blue_border(300, 300)
       move.backward_to_blue_border(300, 300)

How to turn to a specific yaw angle

Pre-Steps:
  • Decide what yaw angle you need to move to. (You need to check hub properties page by clicking the hub icon in the top-left to find this out).
  • Decide whether you want to move the left or right wheel/motor.
To turn the right motor in the left direction to achieve a yaw angle of 150, at a speed of 100: ``` move.right_motor_left_for(100, 150) ``` :warning: **Note that you want to use a slow speed(max 100) for turns to have a higher accuracy.** To turn the right motor in the right direction to achieve a yaw angle of 50, at a speed of 80: ``` move.right_motor_right_for(80, 50) ``` To turn the left motor in the left direction to achieve a yaw angle of 150, at a speed of 100: ``` move.left_motor_left_for(100, 150) ``` To turn the left motor in the right direction to achieve a yaw angle of 50, at a speed of 80: ``` move.left_motor_right_for(80, 50) ``` ### How to's for turning the robot arms #### How to turn the front robot arm To turn the front robot arm by 140 degrees clockwise at a speed of 100: ``` front_arm.run_for_degrees(140, 100) ``` :warning: **Note that you should use a slow speed(max 100) for better accuracy.** To turn the front robot arm by 140 degrees counter-clockwise at a speed of 100: ``` front_arm.run_for_degrees(140, -100) ``` Note that since we are using geared motors, clockwise on the motor turns into counter-clockwise movement of the arm. ### How to's for debugging #### How to set a breakpoint To set a breakpoint activated by the left button: ``` breakpoint(button=button.LEFT) ``` If you add a breakpoint, the robot will pause execution at that point until you press the Left Button.
